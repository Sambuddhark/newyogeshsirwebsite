Superconductor Photonics

Under the broad scope of superconductor terahertz photonics, we exploit the extraordinary potential of high-Tc superconductors in realizing low-loss, fully switchable metamaterials for several exciting and valuable phenomenon, including ultra-high-Q resonances, deep subwavelength sub-London penetration depth thick metamaterials, ultrafast modulators, dual channel ultrafast switches and terahertz superinductors. We also harness the potential of the high-Tc superconductor metamaterials for probing emergent transient phases such as light-induced superconductivity, bosonic metallic states, and charge density waves in quantum solids upon photoexcitation with an optical pulse. The efforts are also underway to develop kinetic inductance-based single photon detectors/ photon counters and Josephson junction/SQUID metamaterials.

Ultrafast Quantum Material Photonics

Under this domain, we integrate the different active quantum materials to the metamaterials and study light-matter interaction to realize the active THz devices, which are facile, cost-effective, and ultrafast in nature. We would control the Light-matter interaction by applying external perturbations, for instance, optical irradiation, electric field and magnetic field, to develop all-optical, electro-optic and magneto-optic terahertz photonic devices.

Dielectric Metasurfaces

In this domain, we design, fabricate and characterize all-dielectric metasurfaces that offer a low-loss platform for efficiently manipulating terahertz waves. These CMOS-compatible THz devices enable all-optical switches with extremely sharp resonances for numerous applications in lasing, mode multiplexing and biosensing.

THz Meta-Sensors

This domain aims to develop ultrasensitive THz sensors for broad range of applications for social issues needs like health, biochemical, environment, astronomy, security, and food, for which THz technology holds promise for disruptive and unique solutions.

THz Spintronics

This work uses an intense femtosecond laser pulse to drive the spins far from equilibrium in a Ferromagnet (FM), which causes ultrafast demagnetization and superdiffusion at sub-picosecond time scales. This process generates the transient spin current in the FM layer, which on entering in a material with high spin-orbit coupling, causes a transient charge current. This transient charge current leads to the emission of the terahertz pulses and opens new pathways for efficient terahertz sources and spin devices.

Probing New Materials

We exploit ultrafast terahertz measurements to investigate a large number of elementary and collective excitations of the correlated materials having phonons, magnons, polaritons, polarons, plasmons, and superconducting gap excitations. We will drive these excitations in these materials using optical pulses and which will be probed using coherent THz waves, leading to the discovery of new phenomena, phases, and functionalities.
